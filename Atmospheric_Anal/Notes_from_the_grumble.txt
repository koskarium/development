3. DATASETS
	MIL-HDBK-310 – Environmental Design Reference

		Purpose: Provides standardized 1%, 5%, 10% exceedance statistics for temperature, humidity, and other environmental parameters to support engineering design.
		Variables / Parameters:
			Dry-bulb temperature: Air temperature at 1.5–2 m above ground
			Wet-bulb temperature: Combines temperature and humidity to indicate human/equipment heat stress
			Relative humidity: Atmospheric moisture content
			Wind speed: Usually averaged over short-term observations
			Solar radiation / thermal loads: Included for extreme design considerations
		Spatial Resolution: 1° × 1° global grid (~111 km), station-interpolated
		Temporal Resolution: Hourly; standardized 31-day month for percentile calculations
		Height: 1.5–2 m above ground (air temperature, wet-bulb)

	ERA5 (ECMWF Reanalysis – Hourly 2 m Air Temperature & More)

		Variables:
			2m_temperature – Near-surface air temperature at 2 m
			2m_specific_humidity – Moisture content of the air at 2 m
			surface_pressure – Atmospheric pressure at surface
			10m_u_component_of_wind – East-west wind component at 10 m
			10m_v_component_of_wind – North-south wind component at 10 m
			skin_temperature – Surface skin temperature
		Spatial: 0.25° × 0.25° (~28 km)
		Temporal: Hourly
		Time Range: 1940–present
		Data Type: Reanalysis
		Height: 2 m (air), 10 m (wind), surface (skin)
		Access: https://cds.climate.copernicus.eu/datasets/reanalysis-era5-single-levels?tab=overview 

	MERRA‑2 (NASA Reanalysis – 2 m Air Temperature & More)

		Variables:
			T2M – 2 m air temperature
			QV2M – 2 m specific humidity
			PS – Surface pressure
			U10M / V10M – 10 m wind components
			TS – Surface skin temperature
		Spatial: 0.5° × 0.625° (~50–70 km)
		Temporal: Hourly
		Time Range: 1980–present
		Data Type: Reanalysis
		Height: 2 m (air), 10 m (wind), surface (skin)
		Access: https://gmao.gsfc.nasa.gov/gmao-products/merra-2/data-access_merra-2/

	MODIS Land Surface Temperature (NASA Satellite)

		Variables:
			LST_1KM – Daily 1 km land surface temperature
		Spatial: 1 km × 1 km
		Temporal: Daily
		Time Range: 2000–present
		Data Type: Satellite
		Height: Surface skin
		Access: https://modis.gsfc.nasa.gov/data/dataprod/mod21.php 

	GHCN-CAMS (NOAA Gridded Daily Surface Air Temperature)

		Variables:
			TAVG – Daily mean air temperature at 2 m
			TMAX – Daily maximum air temperature
			TMIN – Daily minimum air temperature
			PRCP – Daily precipitatio
		Spatial: 0.5° × 0.5
		Temporal: Monthl
		Time Range: 1948–202
		Data Type: Observatio
		Height: 2 m
		Access: https://psl.noaa.gov/data/gridded/data.ghcncams.html

	GHCN-Daily (NOAA Station Observations)

		Variables:
			Maximum / Minimum Air Temperature
			Daily Mean Air Temperature
			Precipitation
		Spatial: Point stations
		Temporal: Daily
		Time Range: 1880–present (varies by station)
		Data Type: Observation
		Height: 2 m
		Access: https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/metadata/landing-page/bin/iso?id=gov.noaa.ncdc:C00861&utm_source=chatgpt.com

5. Proposed Peak-Over-Threshold (POT) with different duration 
	a. POT is widely used in hydrology, climatology, finance, and environmental extremes:
		i. 		https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/2016WR019426
		ii.		https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-022-22243-8
		iii.	https://www.wcrp-climate.org/images/summer_school/Agenda_and_Program_Downloadable_Lectures/Summerschool_Nanjing_lecture_Extreme_value_theory_Hannart.pdf
		iv. 	https://grotjahn.ucdavis.edu/EWEs/extremes_primer_v9_22_15.pdf
	b. Ponteially - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawkes_process
		i. 	Clustering and oscillation period, assuming there is no independence between hot days i.e. today is hot so probably tomorrow will too
	c. POT is the most used method at the moment over Block Maxima, the distribution of values you get out should them follow a generalised Pareto distribution
	d. Duration-based approach
		i. 		Instead of analyzing hourly points, compute rolling means over durations relevant to engineering or human stress:
		ii. 	1h instantaneous extreme
		iii.	6h short-term sustained
		iv. 	24h daily sustained
		v. 		72h multi-day heatwave 
	e.  Threshold selection
		i. 	Choose 1%, 5%, 10% exceedance thresholds for each duration to mimic MIL-310 percentile style.
		ii.	Thresholds are duration-dependent, so a 24h extreme may be lower than a 1h extreme but represents sustained stress.
	f.	Extract exceedances and decluster (optional)
		i. 	Identify all values exceeding the threshold
		ii.	If clustering occurs (e.g., multi-day heatwave), keep only independent events separated by a minimum gap (e.g., 48h) or model clustering with a Hawkes process
	g. Fit Generalized Pareto Distribution (GPD)
	h. Compute return levels
		i. 	Using the GPD fit, calculate return levels for engineering-relevant periods (10, 25, 30 year)
		ii. Provides a probabilistic, physically meaningful extreme estimate rather than just raw percentiles
